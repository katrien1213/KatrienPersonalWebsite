Pump: A Kinetic Exploration of Fertilization

Timeline 2024.12
Tech Stack Arduino, Air Pumps, Plasma Ball
Role Hardware Engineer & Sculptor
Watch Performance

Conceptual Framework: "Building a Home"

This artwork is inspired by the poetic yet painful theory that menstrual pain arises because the womb is "building a home" for new life. Every month, the body tirelessly prepares for the union of egg and sperm. When no fertilization occurs, the womb must dismantle these intricate preparations, resulting in the physical process of bleeding.

"Pump" intends to visualize this cycle of construction, anticipation, and release through kinetic movement and light. It combines fixed sculptural models with Arduino-driven pneumatic devices to simulate the biological rhythm of life.

Biological Context

The interaction logic is based on recent biological studies regarding fertilization. Contrary to the popular myth that the "strongest" sperm wins the race, research suggests that the egg plays an active role in selection.

"It is the egg that actively chooses the proper sperm, rather than the sperm simply winning a race of strength."

To reflect this, the central element (the Egg) remains still and constant, while the surrounding elements (Sperm) attempt to interact with it. The successful interaction is not determined by speed or force, but by a randomized selection logic programmed into the system.

Technical Design & Implementation

The installation uses a combination of light, air inflation (pneumatics), and electricity to symbolize flow and development.

The Egg (Center) Represented by a Plasma Ball. It provides a static but electrically charged core, symbolizing the potential for life.
The Sperm (Surroundings) Represented by 4 rubber balloons. These are connected to air pumps to simulate organic movement (inflation/deflation).
Control System Arduino Uno controlling a relay module for the air pumps and the power state of the plasma ball.
Visual Metaphor Electricity arcs within the plasma ball when a balloon (conductive rubber) touches it, visualizing the "spark" of fertilization.

Interaction Logic

There are four balloons positioned around the central plasma ball. The interaction is programmed to happen randomly.

The Arduino triggers the air pumps to inflate specific balloons at random intervals. As a balloon expands, it physically presses against the glass of the plasma ball. This contact attracts the plasma filaments inside the ball to that specific point of contact, creating a concentrated beam of light—symbolizing the moment of connection and the egg's "choice."
