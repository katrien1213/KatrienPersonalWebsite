Generative Calligraphy: "Grievance"

View Code Watch Prototype

Overview

This interactive project draws inspiration from the calligraphy artwork "Grievance in Prison (七恸歌)" by the renowned Chinese general, Tingbi Xiong (1569-1625). The project transforms traditional calligraphy into a dynamic experience, where audiences write verses on AI-designed postcards. These actions trigger a WebCam classification system, generating real-time ink paintings and visual effects that reflect the historical suffering and unfulfilled aspirations of the Ming Dynasty era.

1. Historical Context & Research

Tingbi Xiong was a national hero of the Ming Dynasty who devoted his life to defending his homeland. Wrongly accused due to political conspiracy, he wrote "Grievance in Prison" before his execution to record his indignation.

"In 2013, Xiong Tingbi's 'Grievance in Prison' was sold for $18.4 million USD, highlighting the enduring significance of his spirit and artistry."

We wish to express Xiong's unwavering integrity in code-generated calligraphy, believing that each stroke of the code symbolizes his tireless efforts for a cause greater than himself. The interactive nature allows the audience to empathize with the bloodshed and tragedy of war depicted in the artwork.

2. Concept & AI Generation

The project utilizes a hybrid workflow of physical interaction and AI generation. We utilized Midjourney to design the postcards that serve as the interface for the audience.

Generative Workflow

After several trials, we refined our prompts to capture the aesthetic of the Ming Dynasty:
"Scroll, Wang Ximeng in East Asian Art, serene blues, elegant greens, and graceful yellow, harmonious balance depicting the Ming Dynasty style."

3. Programming & Logic

The technical core utilizes p5.js for visual generation and ml5.js (Teachable Machine) for gesture recognition. The logic consists of three parts:

A. Machine Learning Recognition

We trained a model to recognize two distinct visual classes via Webcam:

  • Class 1 "Writing": Detects the handwriting side of the postcard.
  • Class 2 "Painting": Detects the illustration side of the postcard.

B. Dynamic Response

if (label == "writing") {
  call calligraphyFunction(); // Generates ink strokes
  call landscapeFunction();
} else if (label == "painting") {
  call bloodParticles(); // Generates dissolve effect
  call landscapeFunction();
}

C. visual Effects

For the code-generated calligraphy, we used noise() algorithms to create organic, ever-changing characters. The blood particle effects utilize nested for-loops to create a dissolving visual metaphor, representing the disintegration of the Ming Dynasty.

4. Challenges & Future Work

Performance Optimization: The p5.js sketch repaints 800 times per frame for the particle effects, leading to network sluggishness on lower-end devices. Future iterations will optimize the nested loop structure or utilize shaders (GLSL) for better performance.

Virtual Reality (VR): We aim to expand this into a VR experience, allowing viewers to step inside the world of calligraphy, looking around and interacting with the floating strokes to enhance immersion.
